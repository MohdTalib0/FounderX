Most SaaS SEO is content farming. You will not win that way against Jasper, Copy.ai, and HubSpot who have 10,000+ indexed pages and DAs above 80.

## 1. Technical SEO Foundation

> These are non-negotiable before any content work.

### Site Architecture
```
wrively.com/                        → Landing (primary conversion)
wrively.com/pricing                 → Pricing
wrively.com/blog/                   → Content hub
wrively.com/tools/                  → Free tools (traffic magnets)
wrively.com/compare/                → Comparison pages
wrively.com/for/                    → Persona landing pages
```

### Core Technical Checklist
- **Core Web Vitals:** LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, INP < 200ms — public pages need SSR or prerendering. Add `@vitejs/plugin-prerender` or migrate public pages to Next.js/Astro
- **Canonical tags:** Every page needs `<link rel="canonical">` — extend to all public routes
- **Sitemap:** `wrively.com/sitemap.xml` — auto-generate, include lastmod dates, submit to Google Search Console
- **robots.txt:** Block `/dashboard`, `/onboarding`, `/settings` — never index authenticated pages
- **Schema markup:** `WebApplication`, `FAQPage` on Pricing, `Article` on all blog posts, `Organization` on homepage
- **Page speed:** Lazy-load all below-the-fold content, preconnect to API origins
- **HTTPS + www redirect:** Force `https://wrively.com` as canonical form
- **404 handling:** Custom page with search and popular links — don't leak equity

### Meta Tag Template (apply to every public page)
```html
<title>{Page Topic} | Wrively</title>
<meta name="description" content="{Specific outcome, 140–155 chars}">
<meta property="og:title" content="{Same as title}">
```

## 9. LinkedIn-Native Distribution (The Channel You're Underusing)

> SEO is a 6-month play. LinkedIn is a day-one play. Your product lives on LinkedIn — your distribution should too.

The current GTM is SEO-heavy. That's correct for long-term compounding. But the fastest path to your first 500 users is not Google — it's the platform your product is built for.

### Channel 1 — Founder-Led Distribution (CRITICAL)
**The strategy:** Post on LinkedIn 3x per week using Wrively. Publicly. Explicitly.

This is not optional. You are your own best case study.

**Weekly post structure (non-negotiable):**
- **Monday — Insight post:** A real observation from building Wrively or the founder LinkedIn space. Written using Wrively, optionally disclosed.
- **Wednesday — Build-in-public post:** Behind the scenes of building — a decision, a failure, a metric, a lesson. The transparency earns trust.
- **Friday — Product-related post:** Show the output, the process, or the result. "I needed a post about X. Here's what Wrively gave me." The product demo IS the content.
- **Every 2 weeks (replaces one of the above) — Proof post:** Before/after, 30-day results, engagement screenshot, user win. See Channel 3a for formats.

Occasionally pull back the curtain explicitly: "I wrote this in 3 minutes using Wrively — here's how." That post will generate more signups than any ad you run.

**Why it works:** Your ICP is on LinkedIn daily. They will see a founder posting consistently, wonder how, and click your profile. Consistency is the signal — they assume you've solved the problem they haven't.

### Channel 3a — The Proof Loop (The Missing Piece)

Category and positioning get attention. Proof closes.

Right now the GTM has strong logic and strong narrative — but no evidence loop. People don't buy a category. They buy results. The proof loop is how you turn "interesting" into "I need to sign up."

**What the proof loop looks like:**

Run one proof post every 2 weeks, permanently. These are the highest-converting posts you will ever publish.

| Proof format | Example |
|---|---|
| Time-based result | "I posted 3x a week for 30 days using Wrively. Here's exactly what happened to my profile." |

**The rule:** One proof post every 2 weeks. No skipping. Proof compounds — post 10 is more credible than post 1 because there's a track record behind it.

**Why proof beats everything else:**
- People don't believe positioning claims — they believe results
- A screenshot of 47 comments on a Wrively-generated post does more work than your entire homepage
- Proof posts get shared — people tag founders they know who "need to see this"
- Each proof post is a reusable asset: screenshot it, put it on the landing page, add it to the pitch deck

**Add to the Brand Language Rules:** Every 2 weeks, a proof post goes out. This is non-negotiable. The proof loop is what turns the Voice Layer from a concept into a belief.

### Channel 3b — "Same Idea, 3 Ways" — Your Viral Distribution Engine

This is the most underused asset in the entire GTM plan. Wrively generates 3 variations of every post — Safe, Bold, and Spicy. That format IS your content strategy.

**The format:**

Post one topic, three ways, in a single LinkedIn post:

> "I had one idea for a post today. Here's what Wrively gave me:
>
> **Safe:** [paste safe version]
>
> **Bold:** [paste bold version]
>
> **Spicy:** [paste spicy version]
>
> I went with Bold. Which one would you have posted?"

**Why this works:**
- It's a live product demo that doesn't feel like a demo
- The question at the end drives comments (LinkedIn algorithm loves this)
- Readers understand the product instantly — better than any explainer page
- It's repeatable every week with a different topic — it never gets old
- The contrast between Safe and Spicy is inherently engaging — people have opinions

**Posting cadence:** Once a week minimum. This is your highest-performing content format. Run it consistently.

**The meta-effect:** After 10 of these posts, your audience starts recognizing "Safe / Bold / Spicy" as Wrively's language. You've trained your ICP's mental model without a single ad.
